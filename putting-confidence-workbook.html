    Reading Greens with Confidence

    Core Insight: Most amateur golfers misread greens because they look for slope without a system. Reading greens is a skill that improves dramatically with the right framework and deliberate repetition.
    Reflection
    Where do you typically misread putts — short, medium, or long? Left-to-right or right-to-left?
    My Green Reading Checklist
    • Read overall slope walking to the green
    • Crouch low behind the ball to see the fall line
    • Check from the low side for true break
    • Identify the high point of the break (apex)
    • Pick a specific spot to aim at (not just a direction)
    • Commit to the read — no second-guessing at address
    Practice Log — Breaking Putts
    Pick a 10-foot breaking putt. Attempt 10 balls. Record your results and observations.

    Today's Drill: Find a putt with at least 2 feet of break at 15 feet. Read it fully using your checklist above. Hit 15 putts, tracking how many times your read was correct (ball started on the right line) vs. how many times you misread it. Separate "bad read" from "bad stroke."

    Segment Three

    Distance Control & Speed Mastery

    Core Insight: Three-putts are almost always a distance problem, not a direction problem. When you control speed, you eliminate the dreaded lag-putt disaster and make your short putts automatic.
    Reflection
    Think about your last round. How many three-putts did you have? Were they caused by poor speed or poor direction?
    On long putts, do you tend to leave them short or blow them past?
    Distance Control Framework
    • I use backstroke length (not force) to control distance
    • I practice lag putting from 30, 40, and 50 feet regularly
    • I can consistently stop the ball within 3 feet from 30 feet
    • I know how different green speeds affect my stroke length
    • I never try to "muscle" a putt — I let the stroke do the work

    Today's Drill — The Ladder Drill: Place balls at 10, 20, 30, 40, and 50 feet from the hole. Putt each one, trying to get within a 3-foot circle. Work back to 50 feet then back to 10. Goal: zero balls outside 3 feet per full ladder. Log how many rounds of the ladder you complete clean.

    Segments Four through Seven

    Setup, Stroke, Short Game & The Mental Game

    Segment 4 · Setup & Alignment
    What setup fundamentals do you struggle with most? (Grip, posture, ball position, eye position)
    • Eyes directly over the ball at address
    • Grip pressure light and consistent — no tension
    • Ball positioned forward in my stance (inside left heel)
    • Putter face square to target line — verified with alignment aid
    • Shoulders, hips, and feet parallel to target line
    Segment 5 · The Putting Stroke
    Does your stroke feel mechanical or natural? What breaks down under pressure?
    • Stroke is driven by shoulders — not hands or wrists
    • Backstroke and follow-through are equal in length
    • Putter stays low through impact — no scooping
    • Head stays still through the entire stroke
    Segment 6 · Pressure Putting (Short Putts)
    At what distance do short putts start to feel pressured for you? What happens in your body?

    Drill: 3-foot circle drill — set 8 tees in a circle 3 feet from the hole. Must make all 8 consecutively to finish. Record how many attempts to complete.
